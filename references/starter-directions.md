# Starter Directions

Use these as first-screen options. Adapt names and sample copy to the user's topic, but keep the differences clear.

## 1. Editorial Think Tank

Best for: business insight, strategy commentary, AI industry analysis, founder/operator notes.

Mood:
- Calm, premium, credible, editorial.
- Looks like a recurring column rather than a decoration pack.

Visual tokens:
- Background: warm white `#FFFDF8`.
- Primary ink: deep green-black `#18231F`.
- Accent: antique gold `#9B7A37`.
- Soft panel: warm sand `#F3EAD7`.
- Border: pale parchment `#E7DFCF`.
- Shape: almost square, radius 0-4px.
- Density: generous margins, short paragraphs, low ornament.

Signature components:
- Issue label: small uppercase line such as `BUSINESS NOTE 042`.
- Title block: large headline with a deep green underline.
- Thesis card: warm sand box with one decisive claim.
- Evidence note: thin border panel with source/date.
- Closing memo: compact final judgment.

Wechat fit:
- Most components work as rich text.
- Hero cover, complex charts, and comparative matrices should be image-based.

## 2. Dark Gold Opinion Column

Best for: contrarian takes, sharp commentary, strong personal opinions, trend criticism.

Mood:
- High contrast, cinematic, direct, memorable.
- More "columnist" than "consultant".

Visual tokens:
- Background: near black `#0B0D10`.
- Panel: charcoal `#111418`.
- Text: warm ivory `#F4EFE3`.
- Accent: gold `#D0A24E`.
- Secondary text: muted stone `#CFC6B8`.
- Shape: hard edges, strong left borders.
- Density: punchy sections, strong quotes, fewer small decorations.

Signature components:
- Opening punchline: one-sentence claim before the title.
- Title block: dark panel with gold divider.
- Sharp quote: left gold border, short and forceful.
- "Reality check" card: concise counterintuitive insight.
- End line: one memorable sentence.

Wechat fit:
- Simple dark cards can work as rich text, but dark full-page backgrounds may paste inconsistently.
- Use image-based modules for the strongest black-gold visual moments.

## 3. Consulting Playbook

Best for: tutorials, frameworks, case breakdowns, workflow guides, operational playbooks.

Mood:
- Clear, structured, practical, calm.
- Feels like a useful field guide rather than a report deck.

Visual tokens:
- Background: clean white `#FFFFFF`.
- Page tint: blue gray `#EEF3F7`.
- Primary text: navy ink `#15202B`.
- Accent: reliable blue `#3268A8`.
- Soft block: ice blue `#E9F2FB`.
- Border: cool gray `#D7E0E8`.
- Shape: 4-6px radius, clear modules.
- Density: scannable, numbered, table-like rhythm.

Signature components:
- Playbook label: `PLAYBOOK / 03`.
- Action title: practical headline.
- Two-column decision cards.
- Step block: numbered method with one outcome per step.
- Checklist CTA: what to do next.

Wechat fit:
- Most components work as rich text if columns are simplified on mobile.
- Complex grids, diagrams, and process maps should become images.

## How To Present

Do not present these as abstract names only. For each option include:

- A short sample headline.
- Palette and mood.
- 3 reusable components.
- What type of author it suits.
- One caveat about WeChat implementation.

Then ask what is closest and what should change.
